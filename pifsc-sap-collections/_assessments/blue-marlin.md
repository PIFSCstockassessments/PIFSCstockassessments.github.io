Indo-Pacific blue marlin (_Makaira mazara_) are found in tropical and subtropical waters of the Pacific Ocean and assessed as a single stock within the Pacific Ocean. Blue marlin are predominantly caught by Japanese, Korean, and Taiwanese high seas longline fisheries. In the 1950s and 1960s, bycatch of blue marlin in Japanese longline fisheries represented the largest portion of blue marlin take. In more recent years, Japanese efforts have decreased while efforts by Taiwanese and other Indo-Pacific countries (Korea, China, Indonesia, and French Polynesia) have increased. 

Because of the species' wide range and long-distance migrations, the stock is assessed by the International Scientific Committee for Tuna and Tuna-like Species in the North Pacific (ISC) [Billfish Working Group]({{wg-partner.href}}) (BILLWG), which is a collaborative effort between multinational and multi-regional fisheries management organizations that conducts assessments and provides scientific advice for swordfish and marlin species.

The most recent benchmark assessment was developed in 2013 and updated in 2016. The 2016 update was an age-, length-, and sex-structured Stock Synthesis model that incorporated sex-specific growth and natural mortality rates to account for sexual dimorphism of adults. The assessment model included time-series data from 1971 to 2014 catch, relative abundance, and length and weight composition from multiple fisheries), and biological information.
